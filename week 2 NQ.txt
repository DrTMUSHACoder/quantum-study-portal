1.Question:Which of the following are Qiskit Runtime primitives currently available
on the IBM Quantum Platform?
Answer: (a) Sampler, (b) Estimator
Explanation:
Qiskit Runtime provides two core primitives for quantum computing:
•Sampler: Executes quantum circuits and returns quasi-probability distribu-
tions of measurement outcomes
•Estimator: Computes expectation values of observables on quantum states
Circuit-Runner and Executor are not official IBM Qiskit Runtime primitives.

69.Question:In a quantum teleportation protocol, Bob applies correction gates based
on the 2-bit classical message from Alice. If Bob’s gate sequences in four trials are:
(I, I), (Z, I), (X, I), and (XZ, I), what are the corresponding 2-bit messages Alice
sent?
Answer: (a) 00, 01, 10, 11
Explanation:
In quantum teleportation, Alice measures two qubits and sends the results (2 classical
bits) to Bob. Bob applies correction gates based on these measurement outcomes:
Measurement (m 1m0)Bob’s Gates Correction
00 I No correction needed
01 Z Phase flip
10 X Bit flip
11 XZ (or ZX) Both flips
The standard teleportation protocol uses:
•m 0= 0: No X gate;m 0= 1: Apply X gate
•m 1= 0: No Z gate;m 1= 1: Apply Z gate
Matching Bob’s gates to messages:
•(I, I)→00
•(Z, I)→01
•(X, I)→10
•(XZ, I)→11
